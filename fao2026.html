Outline

Indonesia country case study · 10 minutes
1. The shock
Oil price trajectory since conflict onset (Feb 28) — Brent +57% to peak; regional fuel price response
2. Transmission channels
Fuel → fertilizer (urea +75%); transport costs; food retail margin; domestic vs world rice price divergence
3. Indonesia's subsidy buffer
Subsidised prices (Pertalite, Solar) frozen all 2026; non-subsidised pass-through delayed ~100 days
4. Fiscal cost of the buffer
Subsidy burden Rp 46–60T/month; total Jan–Jun ~Rp 333T; migration risk from non-subsidised users
5. Distributional implications
Who benefits from the freeze? Coverage gaps; rural smallholder exposure; food price pressure on poor households
6. Is it enough?
Subsidy as first-line SP response; what targeted instruments are still needed?

Imported food inflation risk
Indonesia imports all wheat, ~75% sugar, ~60% soybean & all cotton — a weaker IDR directly amplifies food import costs. With non-subsidised fuel prices now rising, margin pressure on food retailers is intensifying further.
